About Me

I’m a PhD student in Computational Neuroscience at University of Chicago advised by Professors Ramon Nogueira and Dave Freedman. My research focuses on understanding neural data using geometric tools to further investigate mechanism behind cognitive behavior, such as generalization. To this end, I use geometry of representation, representation learning, computational modeling, and different neural recording methods, such as electrophysiology.

I received my BS in Neuroscience at Duke University and MSc in Neuroscience at University College London, where I was advised by Professor Kenneth Harris and Dr. Kevin J Miller.
